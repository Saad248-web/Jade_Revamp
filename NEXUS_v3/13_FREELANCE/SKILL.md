# NEXUS FREELANCE Engine v3.0
**Proposals win work. Contracts protect it. Processes keep it profitable.**

---

## The Freelance Philosophy

Skills gap ≠ income gap. The gap between your skill level and your income is almost always a business system gap — not a talent gap. This engine fixes that.

**Three non-negotiables:**
1. **Scope before you price.** Never quote without understanding the full brief.
2. **Contract before you start.** No exceptions. Ever. Regardless of how well you know the client.
3. **50% upfront.** Every project. No upfront = no protection = no leverage.

## PHASE 1 — Discovery Call Framework (Before Any Proposal)

Use this structure for every first client call (30–45 min):

```
MINUTES 0–5: Relationship
- "Tell me about yourself and the company"
- Let them talk. You listen. You're building trust, not selling yet.

MINUTES 5–20: The Problem
- "What are you trying to achieve with this project?"
- "What does success look like to you?" (Get specific metrics if possible)
- "What's been tried before? Why didn't it work?"
- "Who is the end user?"
- "What's the business impact if this doesn't get done?"

MINUTES 20–30: Constraints
- "What's your timeline?"
- "Do you have a budget in mind?" (Ask directly — saves everyone time)
- "Who are the decision makers I'll be working with?"
- "What does handoff look like?"

MINUTES 30–40: Red flag check
□ Is the scope clear? (If not → don't quote yet)
□ Are there too many stakeholders/opinions?
□ Is the timeline realistic?
□ Does their budget match the scope?
□ Do they respect your time on this call?

MINUTES 40–45: Next steps
- "I'll put together a proposal within [timeframe]"
- Set a clear deadline for their review
- Never end a call without a concrete next step
```

## PHASE 3 — Pricing Models

### Which Model to Use

| Model | Use when | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| **Project rate** | Scope is clear and defined | Scope creep |
| **Hourly rate** | Unclear scope, research-heavy | Unpredictable income |
| **Retainer** | Ongoing work, repeat client | Dependency |
| **Value-based** | Business outcome is clear + significant | Requires deep discovery |

### Pricing Formula (Project Rate)

```
Estimated hours × your hourly rate × 1.3 (buffer) = Base price

Add:
+ Complexity premium (unusual stack, tight deadline): +20–30%
+ Rush premium (< 2 weeks): +50%
+ Exclusivity (can't take other work): +30%

Minimum project size:
Set a floor — never take a project below it.
Anything below your floor costs more in overhead than it earns.
```

### Value-Based Pricing (Advanced)

```
Formula: Client's expected revenue gain × 10%

Example:
Client's landing page conversion rate: 2%
Expected improvement: to 4% (industry benchmark)
Monthly revenue at current rate: $50,000
Revenue gain from doubling conversion: +$50,000/month
Annual gain: $600,000

Your price: $600,000 × 10% = $60,000
(Or: charge what feels uncomfortable, then add 20%)
```

## PHASE 5 — Contract Essentials

**Never start without a signed contract.** Use Docusign, HelloSign, or Bonsai for e-signatures.

### Minimum Contract Clauses

```
1. SCOPE OF WORK
   Exact deliverables + explicit exclusions.
   "Changes to scope will be quoted and approved separately."

2. PAYMENT TERMS
   - 50% upfront (non-refundable)
   - Remaining schedule with clear triggers
   - Late payment penalty: 1.5%/month
   - Work pauses if payment is 7+ days late

3. REVISIONS
   - Number of revision rounds included (recommended: 2)
   - Additional revisions rate per round
   - What constitutes a revision vs. a new request

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
   - IP transfers upon final payment (not before)
   - You retain the right to show work in portfolio
   - Client owns final deliverables after full payment

5. TIMELINE & DEPENDENCIES
   - Timeline starts on deposit + signed contract
   - Client delays don't extend your deadline
   - If client delays > 2 weeks, project must be re-scoped

6. KILL FEE
   - If client cancels: deposit kept + 25% of remaining balance
   - Protects your time allocation

7. WARRANTIES
   - Bug fixes: X days post-launch for bugs (not feature requests)
   - No warranty for client-made changes post-handoff
```

## PHASE 6 — Scope Creep Defense

Scope creep kills margins. Recognize and respond professionally.

**How to identify it:**
- "Can you just quickly add..."
- "While you're in there..."
- "I forgot to mention..."
- "The stakeholders also want..."

**The professional response:**

```
Email template:

Subject: [Project] — Scope Addition: [Item Name]

Hi [Name],

Happy to add [requested item]. This falls outside our agreed scope
([reference the specific section from the contract]).

Here's what that looks like:
- Additional time: ~X hours
- Cost: $XXX
- Impact on timeline: +X days

Let me know how you'd like to proceed, and I can update our agreement.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Never say "I'll just do it this once."** The second you do, the client learns that the scope is negotiable.

## PHASE 7 — Handoff Checklist

```
FILES & ASSETS
□ All source files delivered (Figma, code repo, PSDs)
□ Exported assets organized and labeled
□ Font licenses transferred (if applicable)
□ Stock image licenses confirmed

CODE HANDOFF (if applicable)
□ README with setup instructions
□ Environment variables documented (not the values — the names)
□ Deployment instructions written
□ Third-party integrations documented
□ Admin credentials transferred securely (not by email — use 1Password share)

DESIGN HANDOFF
□ Design file shared with client ownership
□ Component library / design system documented
□ Style guide delivered (colors, fonts, spacing)
□ Unused/draft pages hidden or deleted

KNOWLEDGE TRANSFER
□ Loom walkthrough video recorded (10–15 min) — clients love this
□ How to make common changes documented
□ Support contact for hosting/domain/integrations

SIGN-OFF
□ Client written confirmation: "I accept the deliverables"
□ Final invoice sent upon sign-off
□ Testimonial requested (right after they say they love it — not weeks later)
□ Referral ask (if relationship is strong)
□ Portfolio permission confirmed in writing
```

## PHASE 8 — Client Email Templates

### Following Up on a Proposal (Day 3 after sending)
```
Subject: Re: Proposal for [Project]

Hi [Name],

Wanted to check in on the proposal I sent over on [date].
Do you have any questions, or is there anything you'd like me to clarify?

I'm holding [start date] for this project — happy to chat if helpful.

[Your name]
```

### Handling "Can You Do It Cheaper?"
```
Subject: Re: [Project] — Budget Discussion

Hi [Name],

Appreciate you being direct. I want to make this work for both of us.

A few options:
1. Reduce scope — I can remove [X] to bring it to $[lower price]
2. Extend timeline — if there's flexibility on timeline, I can phase the work
3. Keep as-is — the current scope is priced to deliver [specific outcome]

What would work best for you?

[Your name]
```

### Late Payment
```
Subject: Invoice #[X] — Gentle Reminder

Hi [Name],

Hope you're well. Just a friendly note that Invoice #[X] for $[amount]
was due on [date] and hasn't been received yet.

[Payment link]

Per our agreement, work is paused until payment is received.
Let me know if you have any questions.

[Your name]
```
